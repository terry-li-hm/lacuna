HSBC GROUP AI STANDARD
CHAPTER 5: GENERATIVE AI REQUIREMENTS

CLASSIFICATION: INTERNAL RESTRICTED
VERSION: 2.1 | EFFECTIVE DATE: 1 MARCH 2025
OWNER: AIMS Responsible AI, Group Chief Information Officer
REVIEWED BY: Group AI Review Committee | February 2025

[ILLUSTRATIVE DEMO VERSION — Based on publicly available HSBC governance materials,
PRA SS1/23, and industry practice. Does not represent HSBC's actual internal policies.]

==============================================================================

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This Standard establishes mandatory requirements for the development,
deployment, and ongoing management of Generative AI (GenAI) systems across
all HSBC Group entities globally.

1.2 This Standard applies to all GenAI systems that:
  (a) generate natural language, code, images, or other content outputs;
  (b) are deployed in customer-facing or employee-facing contexts; or
  (c) are used to inform, influence, or determine business decisions.

1.3 This Standard supplements HSBC's Principles for the Ethical Use of Data
and AI (July 2024) and the Group AI Risk Tiering Framework. Requirements
apply in addition to any obligations under HSBC's Model Risk Management
Policy, Operational Resilience Framework, Data Privacy Policy, and applicable
regulatory obligations.

1.4 Where requirements in this Standard conflict with a higher-tier mandatory
obligation (including applicable regulatory requirements), the higher-tier
obligation takes precedence.

==============================================================================

2. DEFINITIONS

GenAI System: Any AI system that generates outputs — including text, code,
images, audio, or structured data — using large language models, diffusion
models, or other foundation model architectures.

Autonomous Output: A GenAI output delivered to a customer, counterparty, or
regulator without mandatory human review before transmission.

High-Risk GenAI System: A GenAI system classified as Tier 1 under the HSBC AI
Risk Tiering Framework, or any system that produces Autonomous Outputs in a
customer-facing context.

Foundation Model: A large-scale AI model trained on broad data that may be
adapted (via fine-tuning, retrieval augmentation, or prompt engineering) for
downstream tasks. Includes models operated by third-party providers
(e.g. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral).

Hallucination: A GenAI output that presents factually incorrect, fabricated,
or misleading information as accurate.

AI System Owner: The named individual accountable for the governance, risk
management, and ongoing compliance of a GenAI system.

==============================================================================

3. PRE-DEPLOYMENT REQUIREMENTS

3.1 All GenAI systems shall be classified under the HSBC AI Risk Tiering
Framework before any deployment to production or user testing beyond the
development team.

3.2 Tier 1 (High Risk) GenAI systems shall obtain formal approval from the AI
Review Committee (ARC) before deployment to production. Approval shall be
documented and retained for a minimum of seven years.

3.3 Tier 2 (Medium Risk) GenAI systems shall obtain formal approval from the
AI Centre of Excellence (AI CoE) review panel before deployment.

3.4 Tier 3 (Low Risk) GenAI systems shall obtain sign-off from the Business
Unit AI Champion and first-line risk function before deployment.

3.5 All GenAI systems shall undergo a documented Responsible AI Assessment
before deployment. The assessment shall evaluate the system against HSBC's
seven ethical AI principles and identify mitigations for any principle where
full compliance cannot be demonstrated.

3.6 Any GenAI system using a Foundation Model provided by a third party shall
complete a Third-Party AI Vendor Assessment (see Section 8) before entering
production.

3.7 A named AI System Owner at Director level or above shall be designated for
each Tier 1 GenAI system before deployment.

==============================================================================

4. TRANSPARENCY AND DISCLOSURE

4.1 Any customer-facing GenAI system shall disclose to customers, at or before
first interaction, that they are engaging with an AI system.

4.2 Disclosure language shall be clear, prominent, and in the primary language
of the customer's jurisdiction. Disclosure shall not be buried in terms and
conditions or accessible only through hyperlinks.

4.3 Where a GenAI system assists a human employee in communications with
customers (co-pilot mode), disclosure shall be made that AI tools are used
in preparing responses, unless the AI contribution is limited to spelling
correction or formatting.

4.4 All GenAI systems used in regulated activities shall maintain output logs
sufficient to reconstruct any specific interaction upon regulatory request.
Logs shall be retained for a minimum of seven years.

4.5 Where a GenAI system produces outputs used to support a material decision
affecting a customer (including credit assessment, fraud determination, or
product recommendation), the system shall record the inputs and outputs
associated with each decision.

4.6 HSBC shall maintain an internal AI system register recording all GenAI
systems in production, their tier classification, approval status, AI System
Owner, and date of last review.

4.7 Where a customer requests explanation of an AI-assisted decision, HSBC
shall provide a meaningful explanation in plain language within five
business days.

==============================================================================

5. HUMAN OVERSIGHT REQUIREMENTS

5.1 No GenAI system shall produce Autonomous Outputs in a customer-facing
context without prior ARC approval of the autonomous deployment mode.

5.2 Tier 1 GenAI systems shall implement mandatory human review for all
outputs that:
  (a) directly determine customer eligibility for a financial product;
  (b) result in account restriction, suspension, or closure; or
  (c) constitute regulatory filings or disclosures.

5.3 All customer-facing GenAI systems shall implement a human escalation
pathway. Where a customer requests human assistance, escalation shall be
accessible at any point in the interaction. HSBC shall ensure customers are
not required to interact solely with an AI system against their preference.

5.4 Employee-facing GenAI systems used in regulated activities shall
incorporate controls to prevent employees from presenting AI-generated
content to customers without review. These controls shall be documented in
the system's governance record.

5.5 GenAI systems used to support model risk management or compliance
activities shall have their outputs reviewed by a qualified subject matter
expert before any regulatory submission or material decision.

5.6 AI System Owners shall document and periodically report the human override
rate for Tier 1 GenAI systems. Sustained override rates above 20% shall
trigger a formal review of system quality.

==============================================================================

6. DATA QUALITY AND PRIVACY

6.1 All training data, retrieval corpora, and fine-tuning datasets used by
GenAI systems shall be subject to documented data quality assessment before
use. The assessment shall address representativeness, recency, completeness,
and known biases.

6.2 GenAI systems shall not process Special Category Personal Data (as defined
under GDPR/UK GDPR) or equivalent sensitive data categories without:
  (a) a lawful basis documented in a Data Privacy Impact Assessment; and
  (b) Tier 1 governance approval.

6.3 GenAI systems shall implement controls to prevent customer data from being
used to train or improve third-party Foundation Models without explicit
customer consent.

6.4 Where GenAI systems operate with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG),
the retrieval corpus shall be subject to data governance controls equivalent
to those applied to the system's primary inputs.

6.5 GenAI systems shall be assessed for bias against protected characteristics
before deployment. Bias assessment results shall be documented and reviewed
at each periodic review cycle.

6.6 Data used in GenAI systems shall be subject to data minimisation
principles. GenAI systems shall not retain customer interaction data beyond
the period required for the documented business purpose.

==============================================================================

7. MODEL DOCUMENTATION AND VALIDATION

7.1 All Tier 1 and Tier 2 GenAI systems shall maintain a Model Card
documenting:
  (a) intended purpose and scope of use;
  (b) foundation model identity and version;
  (c) training data sources and reference dates;
  (d) known limitations and failure modes;
  (e) bias assessment results;
  (f) human oversight controls; and
  (g) monitoring approach and metrics.

7.2 Model Cards shall be updated at each material change, including foundation
model version upgrades, significant prompt engineering changes, and retrieval
corpus updates.

7.3 Tier 1 GenAI systems shall undergo independent model validation before
deployment to production, consistent with HSBC's Model Risk Management
Policy and aligned to PRA SS1/23. Validation shall include adversarial
testing to assess hallucination rates and prompt injection susceptibility.

7.4 Where a third-party Foundation Model is updated by the provider, the AI
System Owner shall assess whether the update requires re-validation before
continuing production use.

7.5 Hallucination rate benchmarks shall be established for each Tier 1 and
Tier 2 system at deployment. Where the hallucination rate exceeds the
approved threshold in production, the AI System Owner shall initiate a
formal review within five business days.

==============================================================================

8. THIRD-PARTY AND VENDOR REQUIREMENTS

8.1 All GenAI systems using third-party Foundation Models shall complete a
Third-Party AI Vendor Assessment before deployment, addressing:
  (a) vendor AI governance maturity;
  (b) data processing and retention practices;
  (c) model update notification procedures; and
  (d) incident response obligations.

8.2 Contracts with third-party AI providers shall require the provider to:
  (a) notify HSBC of material model updates with a minimum of 30 days notice;
  (b) provide model documentation upon request; and
  (c) cooperate with HSBC's regulatory audit requirements.

8.3 HSBC shall maintain a concentration risk register for third-party AI
dependencies. Where more than 30% of production GenAI systems depend on a
single provider, the Group Chief Information Officer shall be notified.

8.4 Third-party AI vendors shall be subject to ongoing due diligence
consistent with HSBC's Third-Party Risk Management Policy and PRA SS2/21
outsourcing requirements.

==============================================================================

9. MONITORING AND ONGOING COMPLIANCE

9.1 All production GenAI systems shall be subject to performance monitoring.
Monitoring cadence shall be:
  - Monthly for Tier 1 systems
  - Quarterly for Tier 2 systems
  - Annual self-attestation for Tier 3 systems

9.2 Monitoring for Tier 1 and Tier 2 systems shall include, at minimum:
  (a) output quality sampling;
  (b) hallucination rate tracking;
  (c) bias indicator monitoring; and
  (d) human override rate where applicable.

9.3 All GenAI systems shall undergo a formal periodic review:
  - Annually for Tier 1 systems
  - Every two years for Tier 2 systems
  - Every three years for Tier 3 systems

9.4 Material changes to a GenAI system shall trigger an expedited review to
assess whether the change alters the risk tier or requires re-approval.

9.5 The AIMS Responsible AI team shall produce a consolidated GenAI Monitoring
Report for the AI Review Committee at least quarterly, summarising
production incidents, monitoring exceptions, and emerging risk indicators.

Note: Systemic risk monitoring obligations arising from HKMA's proposed AI
Systemic Risk Framework and equivalent multi-jurisdictional reporting
requirements are under review and will be addressed in a subsequent
revision of this Standard.

==============================================================================

10. INCIDENT MANAGEMENT

10.1 An AI incident is defined as any unintended, harmful, or unexpected
output from a GenAI system that results in: customer harm, financial loss,
reputational damage, regulatory breach, or data loss.

10.2 AI incidents shall be reported to the AI System Owner and the AIMS
Responsible AI team within 24 hours of identification.

10.3 Tier 1 GenAI incidents shall be escalated to the Group AI Review
Committee within two business days. The AI System Owner shall produce a
root cause analysis within 10 business days.

10.4 GenAI systems involved in a confirmed incident shall be subject to
suspension from production pending root cause analysis, unless the AI System
Owner and AIMS Responsible AI team jointly determine that continued
operation is appropriate with documented rationale.

10.5 HSBC shall report AI incidents to relevant regulators in accordance with
applicable regulatory notification obligations. Specific notification
timelines are determined by jurisdiction-level regulatory requirements and
are maintained separately by Group Compliance. [Jurisdiction-specific
notification timelines for AI incidents are under review for alignment with
emerging regulatory guidance and will be specified in a subsequent annex.]

10.6 Lessons learned from AI incidents shall be shared across Business Unit
AI Champions within 30 days of root cause analysis completion.

==============================================================================

11. AGENTIC AI SYSTEMS

11.1 An Agentic AI System is a GenAI system that executes multi-step tasks
with limited human intervention, including systems that:
  (a) access external APIs or tools autonomously;
  (b) execute actions with real-world consequences (e.g. sending
      communications, executing transactions, modifying data); or
  (c) chain multiple AI model calls or spawn sub-agents.

11.2 All Agentic AI Systems shall be classified as Tier 1 and subject to full
ARC approval regardless of composite risk score.

11.3 Agentic AI Systems shall implement explicit scope constraints limiting:
  (a) categories of action the system may take autonomously;
  (b) maximum financial exposure per autonomous action; and
  (c) the ability to modify the system's own scope or permissions.

11.4 Human checkpoint requirements for Agentic AI Systems shall be defined at
the design stage and documented in the governance record. Checkpoints shall
be required before any irreversible action.
