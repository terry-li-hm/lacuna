MERIDIAN DEMO BASELINE
ILLUSTRATIVE AI REQUIREMENTS SET — CAPCO-AUTHORED

This document is a synthetic illustrative baseline for demonstrating
paragraph-level gap analysis against regulatory circulars. Requirements are
based on publicly available frameworks including HSBC's published Principles
for the Ethical Use of Data and AI (July 2024), PRA SS1/23, NIST AI RMF,
and industry practice. No internal HSBC documents were used.

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This illustrative requirements set establishes notional mandatory
requirements for the development, deployment, and ongoing management of
Generative AI (GenAI) systems within a hypothetical financial institution
operating across multiple regulated jurisdictions.

1.2 This baseline applies to all GenAI systems that:
  (a) generate natural language, code, images, or other content outputs;
  (b) are deployed in customer-facing or employee-facing contexts; or
  (c) are used to inform, influence, or determine business decisions.

1.3 Requirements supplement notional ethical AI principles and risk
tiering methodology. They apply in addition to any obligations under model
risk management policy, operational resilience frameworks, data privacy
policy, and applicable regulatory obligations.

1.4 Where requirements conflict with a higher-tier mandatory obligation
(including applicable regulatory requirements), the higher-tier obligation
takes precedence.

2. DEFINITIONS

GenAI System: Any AI system that generates outputs — including text, code,
images, audio, or structured data — using large language models, diffusion
models, or other foundation model architectures.

Autonomous Output: A GenAI output delivered to a customer, counterparty, or
regulator without mandatory human review before transmission.

Higher-Risk GenAI System: A GenAI system that produces Autonomous Outputs
in a customer-facing context, or that is classified at the highest risk tier
under the applicable risk tiering framework.

Foundation Model: A large-scale AI model trained on broad data that may be
adapted (via fine-tuning, retrieval augmentation, or prompt engineering) for
downstream tasks. Includes models operated by third-party providers.

Hallucination: A GenAI output that presents factually incorrect, fabricated,
or misleading information as accurate.

AI System Owner: The named individual accountable for the governance, risk
management, and ongoing compliance of a GenAI system.

3. PRE-DEPLOYMENT REQUIREMENTS

3.1 All GenAI systems shall be classified under a formal risk assessment
process before any deployment to production or user testing beyond the
development team.

3.2 Higher-risk GenAI systems shall obtain formal approval from the AI
governance review body before deployment to production. Approval shall be
documented and retained for a minimum of seven years.

3.3 Medium-risk GenAI systems shall obtain formal approval from a delegated
AI review panel before deployment.

3.4 Lower-risk GenAI systems shall obtain sign-off from the business unit
risk function before deployment.

3.5 All GenAI systems shall undergo a documented Responsible AI Assessment
before deployment. The assessment shall evaluate the system against the
institution's ethical AI principles and identify mitigations for any
principle where full compliance cannot be demonstrated.

3.6 Any GenAI system using a Foundation Model provided by a third party
shall complete a Third-Party AI Vendor Assessment before entering
production.

3.7 A named AI System Owner at an appropriately senior level shall be
designated for each higher-risk GenAI system before deployment.

3.8 The risk assessment required under 3.1 shall evaluate materiality
across three dimensions:
  (a) Impact — the severity of potential harm to customers, the
      institution, or market integrity if the system fails or produces
      harmful outputs;
  (b) Complexity — the technical complexity of the system, including
      degree of autonomy, multi-step chaining, and opacity of outputs;
  (c) Reliance — the degree to which business decisions or customer
      outcomes depend on the system's outputs without independent
      verification.
The combination of scores across these dimensions shall determine risk
tier assignment and the applicable governance pathway.

3.9 The business case for any higher-risk or medium-risk GenAI system
shall include a total cost of ownership (TCO) analysis addressing:
  (a) current and projected API or infrastructure costs over the
      expected operational lifetime of the system;
  (b) a pricing sensitivity scenario modelling the impact of a
      material cost increase (minimum 3x baseline) on the viability
      of the system;
  (c) estimated switching costs if the primary vendor or model is
      replaced, including re-engineering, retraining, and workflow
      change costs; and
  (d) the point at which vendor lock-in makes migration economically
      impractical.
TCO analyses shall be reviewed at each periodic governance review and
updated where material changes in vendor pricing or market conditions
have occurred.

4. TRANSPARENCY AND DISCLOSURE

4.1 Any customer-facing GenAI system shall disclose to customers, at or
before first interaction, that they are engaging with an AI system.

4.2 Disclosure language shall be clear, prominent, and in the primary
language of the customer's jurisdiction. Disclosure shall not be buried in
terms and conditions or accessible only through hyperlinks.

4.3 Where a GenAI system assists a human employee in communications with
customers (co-pilot mode), disclosure shall be made that AI tools are used
in preparing responses, unless the AI contribution is limited to spelling
correction or formatting.

4.4 All GenAI systems used in regulated activities shall maintain output
logs sufficient to reconstruct any specific interaction upon regulatory
request. Logs shall be retained for a minimum of seven years.

4.5 Where a GenAI system produces outputs used to support a material
decision affecting a customer (including credit assessment, fraud
determination, or product recommendation), the system shall record the
inputs and outputs associated with each decision.

4.6 The institution shall maintain an internal AI system register recording
all GenAI systems in production, their risk classification, approval status,
AI System Owner, and date of last review.

4.7 Where a customer requests explanation of an AI-assisted decision, the
institution shall provide a meaningful explanation in plain language within
five business days.

5. HUMAN OVERSIGHT REQUIREMENTS

5.1 No GenAI system shall produce Autonomous Outputs in a customer-facing
context without prior governance body approval of the autonomous deployment
mode.

5.2 Higher-risk GenAI systems shall implement mandatory human review for
all outputs that:
  (a) directly determine customer eligibility for a financial product;
  (b) result in account restriction, suspension, or closure; or
  (c) constitute regulatory filings or disclosures.

5.3 All customer-facing GenAI systems shall implement a human escalation
pathway. Where a customer requests human assistance, escalation shall be
accessible at any point in the interaction. The institution shall ensure
customers are not required to interact solely with an AI system against
their preference.

5.4 Employee-facing GenAI systems used in regulated activities shall
incorporate controls to prevent employees from presenting AI-generated
content to customers without review. These controls shall be documented in
the system's governance record.

5.5 GenAI systems used to support model risk management or compliance
activities shall have their outputs reviewed by a qualified subject matter
expert before any regulatory submission or material decision.

5.6 AI System Owners shall document and periodically report the human
override rate for higher-risk GenAI systems. Sustained override rates above
20% shall trigger a formal review of system quality.

6. DATA QUALITY AND PRIVACY

6.1 All training data, retrieval corpora, and fine-tuning datasets used by
GenAI systems shall be subject to documented data quality assessment before
use. The assessment shall address representativeness, recency, completeness,
and known biases.

6.2 GenAI systems shall not process Special Category Personal Data or
equivalent sensitive data categories without a lawful basis documented in a
Data Privacy Impact Assessment and appropriate governance approval.

6.3 GenAI systems shall implement controls to prevent customer data from
being used to train or improve third-party Foundation Models without
explicit customer consent.

6.4 Where GenAI systems operate with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG),
the retrieval corpus shall be subject to data governance controls equivalent
to those applied to the system's primary inputs.

6.5 GenAI systems shall be assessed for bias against protected
characteristics before deployment. Bias assessment results shall be
documented and reviewed at each periodic review cycle.

6.6 Data used in GenAI systems shall be subject to data minimisation
principles. GenAI systems shall not retain customer interaction data beyond
the period required for the documented business purpose.

7. MODEL DOCUMENTATION AND VALIDATION

7.1 All higher-risk and medium-risk GenAI systems shall maintain a Model
Card documenting:
  (a) intended purpose and scope of use;
  (b) foundation model identity and version;
  (c) training data sources and reference dates;
  (d) known limitations and failure modes;
  (e) bias assessment results;
  (f) human oversight controls; and
  (g) monitoring approach and metrics.

7.2 Model Cards shall be updated at each material change, including
foundation model version upgrades, significant prompt engineering changes,
and retrieval corpus updates.

7.3 Higher-risk GenAI systems shall undergo independent model validation
before deployment to production. Validation shall include adversarial
testing to assess hallucination rates and prompt injection susceptibility.

7.4 Where a third-party Foundation Model is updated by the provider, the
AI System Owner shall assess whether the update requires re-validation
before continuing production use.

7.5 Hallucination rate benchmarks shall be established for each higher-risk
and medium-risk system at deployment. Where the hallucination rate exceeds
the approved threshold in production, the AI System Owner shall initiate a
formal review within five business days.

8. THIRD-PARTY AND VENDOR REQUIREMENTS

8.1 All GenAI systems using third-party Foundation Models shall complete a
Third-Party AI Vendor Assessment before deployment, addressing:
  (a) vendor AI governance maturity;
  (b) data processing and retention practices;
  (c) model update notification procedures;
  (d) incident response obligations;
  (e) geopolitical availability risk — assessment of whether the vendor's
      continued availability could be impaired by government restrictions,
      export controls, sanctions, or politically motivated access
      prohibitions relevant to the institution's operating jurisdictions,
      including plausible scenarios under which the vendor becomes
      unavailable to regulated entities, and the institution's documented
      contingency plan for each material scenario; and
  (f) vendor ethical alignment — assessment of the vendor's published
      usage policies, ethical red lines, and acceptable use restrictions
      to confirm they are compatible with the institution's intended use
      cases, including any regulated activities (AML, fraud, credit
      decisioning, surveillance). Where a vendor's ethical constraints
      restrict a material intended use case, the institution shall
      document the restriction and identify an alternative approach
      before proceeding to deployment.

8.2 Contracts with third-party AI providers shall require the provider to:
  (a) notify the institution of material model updates with a minimum of
      30 days notice;
  (b) provide model documentation upon request; and
  (c) cooperate with the institution's regulatory audit requirements.

8.3 The institution shall maintain a concentration risk register for
third-party AI dependencies. Where more than 30% of production GenAI
systems depend on a single provider, senior risk oversight shall be
notified.

8.4 Third-party AI vendors shall be subject to ongoing due diligence
consistent with applicable outsourcing and third-party risk frameworks.

8.5 Contracts with third-party AI providers shall require the provider
to notify the institution of any material change to usage policies,
acceptable use restrictions, or availability of the AI system that may
affect the institution's ability to operate regulated services. Where a
provider restricts or prohibits a use case after deployment, the
institution shall have a documented contingency plan enabling continuity
of service within 30 days. The institution shall maintain a vendor
concentration risk review that explicitly addresses regulatory and
geopolitical risks that could impair vendor availability.

9. MONITORING AND ONGOING COMPLIANCE

9.1 All production GenAI systems shall be subject to performance monitoring.
Monitoring cadence shall be proportionate to risk classification:
  - Monthly for higher-risk systems
  - Quarterly for medium-risk systems
  - Annual self-attestation for lower-risk systems

9.2 Monitoring for higher-risk and medium-risk systems shall include, at
minimum:
  (a) output quality sampling;
  (b) hallucination rate tracking;
  (c) bias indicator monitoring; and
  (d) human override rate where applicable.

9.3 All GenAI systems shall undergo a formal periodic review proportionate
to risk classification. Reviews shall confirm that the risk tier remains
appropriate and that the Responsible AI Assessment remains current.

9.4 Material changes to a GenAI system shall trigger an expedited review
to assess whether the change alters the risk classification or requires
re-approval.

9.5 The AI governance function shall produce a consolidated GenAI
monitoring report for the senior oversight body at least quarterly,
summarising production incidents, monitoring exceptions, and emerging
risk indicators.

Note: Requirements for systemic risk reporting to regulators at portfolio
level are under review and will be addressed in a subsequent revision
following finalisation of relevant regulatory guidance.

10. INCIDENT MANAGEMENT

10.1 An AI incident is defined as any unintended, harmful, or unexpected
output from a GenAI system that results in: customer harm, financial loss,
reputational damage, regulatory breach, or data loss.

10.2 AI incidents shall be reported to the AI System Owner and the AI
governance function within 24 hours of identification.

10.3 Higher-risk GenAI incidents shall be escalated to the senior AI
oversight body within two business days. The AI System Owner shall produce
a root cause analysis within 10 business days.

10.4 GenAI systems involved in a confirmed incident shall be subject to
suspension from production pending root cause analysis, unless the AI
System Owner and AI governance function jointly determine that continued
operation is appropriate with documented rationale.

10.5 The institution shall report AI incidents to relevant regulators in
accordance with applicable regulatory notification obligations. Specific
notification timelines vary by jurisdiction and are determined by applicable
regulatory requirements. [Note: Jurisdiction-specific AI incident
notification timelines remain under review pending finalisation of
regulatory guidance in key markets.]

10.6 Lessons learned from AI incidents shall be shared across business
units within 30 days of root cause analysis completion.

11. AGENTIC AI SYSTEMS

11.1 An Agentic AI System is a GenAI system that executes multi-step tasks
with limited human intervention, including systems that:
  (a) access external APIs or tools autonomously;
  (b) execute actions with real-world consequences (e.g. sending
      communications, executing transactions, modifying data); or
  (c) chain multiple AI model calls or spawn sub-agents.

11.2 All Agentic AI Systems shall be classified at the highest risk tier
and subject to full governance body approval regardless of other risk
scoring.

11.3 Agentic AI Systems shall implement explicit scope constraints limiting:
  (a) categories of action the system may take autonomously;
  (b) maximum financial exposure per autonomous action; and
  (c) the ability to modify the system's own scope or permissions.

11.4 Human checkpoint requirements for Agentic AI Systems shall be defined
at the design stage and documented in the governance record. Checkpoints
shall be required before any irreversible action.

No internal HSBC documents were used or referenced in drafting this
baseline.

APPENDIX B: REGULATORY COVERAGE NOTES

This baseline was designed to partially cover requirements from the
following regulatory sources, for use as a gap analysis demonstration:

- HKMA: GenAI Consumer Protection Circular (August 2024) — partial coverage
  by design; gaps are intentional for demonstration
- HKMA: SPM on AI in Financial Services (CA-G-1)

The following regulatory requirements are NOT incorporated in this
illustrative baseline and will show as gaps in gap analysis:

- 2019 BDAI Guiding Principles (explicit extension to GenAI not referenced)
- EU AI Act Articles 9-15 (high-risk system documentation requirements)
- Jurisdiction-specific incident notification timelines
- MAS AIRM: Board-level AI risk reporting cadence and MI pack requirements
- MAS AIRM: AI system inventory completeness attestation obligations

This is intentional for demonstration purposes — these gaps illustrate the
tool's ability to identify where a real policy would require amendment.

MAS AIRM (November 2025) is partially incorporated in this baseline:
requirements 3.8 (materiality assessment), 8.5 (usage policy stability),
6.5 (bias assessment), and 9.1-9.2 (monitoring cadence) align with MAS
lifecycle control expectations. Gaps are intentional — the baseline does
not yet fully cover MAS AIRM governance and board-level reporting
obligations.
