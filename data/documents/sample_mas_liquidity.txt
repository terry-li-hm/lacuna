Monetary Authority of Singapore
Notice 652 - Liquidity Framework
Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)
Issued: March 2024

1. Overview

This Notice sets out the requirements for banks incorporated in Singapore to maintain adequate liquidity through compliance with the Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR).

2. Scope of Application

2.1 Covered Institutions
This Notice applies to all banks incorporated in Singapore, including full banks, wholesale banks, and merchant banks.

2.2 Consolidated Basis
Banks must comply with LCR requirements on both a solo entity and consolidated basis.

3. Minimum LCR Requirement

3.1 Standard Requirement
Banks must maintain an LCR of at least 100% at all times, calculated as:

LCR = High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA) / Total Net Cash Outflows over 30 days

3.2 Enhanced Requirement
Domestic systemically important banks (D-SIBs) designated by MAS may be subject to higher LCR requirements on a case-by-case basis.

4. High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA)

4.1 Level 1 Assets
Level 1 assets receive 100% weighting and include:
- Cash
- Central bank reserves
- Singapore Government Securities (SGS)
- MAS bills

4.2 Level 2A Assets
Level 2A assets receive 85% weighting (15% haircut) and include:
- AAA to AA- rated sovereign securities
- AAA to AA- rated covered bonds
- AAA to AA- rated corporate debt securities

4.3 Level 2B Assets
Level 2B assets receive weightings between 50% to 75% and include:
- BBB+ to BBB- rated corporate bonds (50% haircut)
- Residential mortgage-backed securities rated AA+ or higher (75% weighting)
- Common equity shares (50% haircut)

4.4 Composition Limits
- Level 2 assets cannot exceed 40% of total HQLA
- Level 2B assets cannot exceed 15% of total HQLA

5. Cash Outflows

5.1 Retail Deposits
- Stable retail deposits: 5% outflow rate
- Less stable retail deposits: 10% outflow rate

5.2 Wholesale Funding
- Operational deposits from non-financial corporates: 25% outflow rate
- Non-operational deposits from non-financial corporates: 40% outflow rate
- Unsecured wholesale funding from financial institutions: 100% outflow rate

5.3 Secured Funding
- Transactions with central banks: 0% outflow
- Transactions backed by Level 1 HQLA: 0% outflow
- Transactions backed by Level 2A HQLA: 15% outflow
- Transactions backed by other collateral: 100% outflow

5.4 Additional Requirements
- Committed credit and liquidity facilities: 5% to 100% depending on counterparty type
- Contractual obligations: 100% outflow
- Derivatives cash outflows: 100% of net outflows

6. Cash Inflows

6.1 Inflow Cap
Total cash inflows are capped at 75% of total cash outflows, ensuring banks maintain a minimum level of HQLA.

6.2 Inflow Rates
- Retail lending: 50% inflow rate
- Wholesale lending to non-financial corporates: 50% inflow rate
- Operational deposits at other financial institutions: 0% inflow
- Secured lending backed by Level 1 HQLA: 0% inflow
- Other secured lending: 50% inflow rate

7. Currency Mismatch

7.1 Foreign Currency Exposures
Banks with significant foreign currency exposures must maintain adequate HQLA in each significant currency, defined as 5% or more of total liabilities.

7.2 Minimum Requirement
For each significant currency, banks must maintain sufficient HQLA to cover at least 75% of net cash outflows in that currency.

8. Reporting Requirements

8.1 Frequency
Banks must report their LCR positions to MAS on a daily basis for solo entity and consolidated levels.

8.2 Submission Timeline
Daily LCR returns (MAS Form LCR-1) must be submitted to MAS within 1 business day.

8.3 Quarterly Detailed Returns
Comprehensive LCR returns with detailed breakdowns must be submitted quarterly within 20 business days after quarter-end.

9. Disclosure Requirements

9.1 Public Disclosure
Banks must publicly disclose their average LCR and HQLA composition on a quarterly basis.

9.2 Template Format
Disclosures must follow the common disclosure template specified in Annex A of this Notice.

10. Internal Liquidity Adequacy Assessment Process (ILAAP)

10.1 Requirement
Banks must establish and maintain a robust ILAAP that identifies, measures, monitors, and controls liquidity risk.

10.2 Board Oversight
The Board of Directors must review and approve the ILAAP at least annually.

10.3 Stress Testing
Banks must conduct regular liquidity stress tests covering both bank-specific and market-wide scenarios, with results reviewed by senior management and the Board.

11. Supervisory Review

11.1 MAS Assessment
MAS will assess each bank's liquidity risk management and compliance with LCR requirements through ongoing supervision.

11.2 Additional Requirements
MAS may impose higher LCR requirements or additional liquidity conditions based on a bank's risk profile, business model, or systemic importance.

12. Remedial Measures

12.1 Breach Notification
Banks must immediately notify MAS if they breach or anticipate breaching the minimum LCR requirement.

12.2 Restoration Plan
Upon breach, banks must submit a detailed restoration plan to MAS within 5 business days, outlining actions to restore compliance.

12.3 Supervisory Actions
MAS may take supervisory actions including restrictions on dividend distributions, business activities, or requirements to raise additional capital or liquidity.

13. Effective Date

13.1 Implementation
This Notice came into full effect on January 1, 2015, replacing earlier interim liquidity requirements.

13.2 Ongoing Updates
MAS reviews and updates liquidity requirements periodically to reflect international standards and local market conditions.
