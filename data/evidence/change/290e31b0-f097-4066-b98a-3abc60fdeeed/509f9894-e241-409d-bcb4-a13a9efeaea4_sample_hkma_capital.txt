Hong Kong Monetary Authority
Supervisory Policy Manual - CA-G-5
Capital Adequacy Ratio
Issued: January 2024

1. Introduction

This module sets out the HKMA's approach to the calculation and maintenance of capital adequacy ratios for authorized institutions (AIs) in Hong Kong.

2. Minimum Capital Requirements

2.1 Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Capital Ratio
All authorized institutions must maintain a minimum CET1 capital ratio of 4.5% of risk-weighted assets at all times.

2.2 Tier 1 Capital Ratio
The minimum Tier 1 capital ratio requirement is 6.0% of risk-weighted assets.

2.3 Total Capital Ratio
The minimum total capital ratio requirement is 8.0% of risk-weighted assets.

3. Capital Conservation Buffer

3.1 Buffer Requirement
In addition to the minimum capital requirements, AIs are required to maintain a capital conservation buffer of 2.5% of risk-weighted assets, composed of CET1 capital.

3.2 Consequences of Breaching Buffer
AIs that do not maintain the capital conservation buffer will face constraints on distributions including dividend payments, share buybacks, and discretionary bonus payments to staff.

4. Countercyclical Capital Buffer

4.1 Buffer Range
The countercyclical capital buffer requirement can range from 0% to 2.5% of risk-weighted assets, depending on prevailing macroeconomic conditions in Hong Kong.

4.2 Current Requirement
As of January 2024, the countercyclical capital buffer is set at 1.0% for exposures to Hong Kong.

5. Systemic Risk Buffer

5.1 Domestic Systemically Important Authorized Institutions (D-SIBs)
D-SIBs are required to maintain an additional capital buffer ranging from 1.0% to 3.5% of risk-weighted assets, depending on their systemic importance.

5.2 Designation Process
The HKMA reviews and updates the list of D-SIBs annually based on quantitative indicators and supervisory judgment.

6. Leverage Ratio

6.1 Minimum Requirement
All AIs must maintain a leverage ratio of at least 3.0%, calculated as Tier 1 capital divided by total exposures.

6.2 Enhanced Requirement for D-SIBs
D-SIBs are subject to an enhanced leverage ratio requirement of 3.75%.

7. Reporting Requirements

7.1 Quarterly Returns
AIs must submit capital adequacy returns (Return MA(BS)1) to the HKMA on a quarterly basis, within 6 weeks after the end of each quarter.

7.2 Annual Disclosures
AIs must make public disclosures of their capital adequacy positions annually, in accordance with Pillar 3 disclosure requirements.

8. Remedial Actions

8.1 Early Warning Triggers
The HKMA will take early supervisory action when an AI's capital ratios fall below the sum of minimum requirements plus buffers.

8.2 Restoration Plans
AIs approaching minimum capital requirements must submit detailed capital restoration plans to the HKMA for approval.

9. Internal Capital Adequacy Assessment Process (ICAAP)

9.1 Requirement
All AIs must maintain a robust ICAAP that identifies, measures, and aggregates all material risks and determines adequate internal capital to support those risks.

9.2 Supervisory Review
The HKMA conducts regular reviews of AIs' ICAAPs and may impose higher capital requirements based on identified deficiencies.

10. Transitional Arrangements

10.1 Implementation Timeline
The full requirements under Basel III came into effect in Hong Kong on January 1, 2019, with certain transitional arrangements for capital instruments.

10.2 Grandfathering Provisions
Non-compliant capital instruments issued before 2013 are subject to phase-out arrangements, with full phase-out completed by January 1, 2022.
